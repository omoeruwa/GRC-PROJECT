### Our Goal

The primary objective of this assessment is to evaluate the security and operational resilience of the Vuln-Bank platform. We are looking beyond just finding bugs. Our goal is to provide a clear view of how technical weaknesses translate into real-world business risks. This report serves as a roadmap for decision makers to understand where the bank is vulnerable and how we can best protect our customers, our financial data, and our reputation.

### Why This Matters

Financial institutions are high-value targets for attackers. A single security gap can lead to unauthorized access, theft of funds, or a leak of private customer information. More importantly, operating a bank requires strict adherence to data protection laws like the NDPR and GDPR. If we fail to secure the platform, we risk heavy fines, legal action, and a total loss of trust from the people who rely on us.

### Defining Our Risk Appetite

In this project, we are operating under a "Low Risk" appetite. This means that for a financial ecosystem, we assume the organization cannot tolerate a high chance of a data breach or financial loss. We are not just looking for "perfect" security, which is impossible. We are looking for "responsible" security. We want to ensure that if a threat does appear, the bank has the necessary controls to detect, stop, and recover from it without the business collapsing.

### Scope of the Assessment

We are analyzing the Vuln-Bank platform as an integrated system. This includes:

* The front-end user experience and authentication flow.
* The back-end database and transaction processing logic.
* The AI customer support agent and its integration with our data.
* The server infrastructure and container environment.

This assessment is meant to be a living document. It will help us prioritize our engineering efforts so that we spend our time and money fixing the problems that actually put the bank at the most risk.
