Engagement Overview
This six-week corporate compliance and AI governance audit engagement, led by Stephen Adeoye, GRC Professional, was tasked with conducting a full-scale assessment of the vuln-bank Digital Ecosystem. The primary objective was to evaluate the platform's current security posture, identify critical vulnerabilities, and provide a roadmap for remediation in alignment with industry frameworks.

Key Findings
Critical Vulnerabilities: The audit identified three "Critical" risks—SQL Injection, Broken Object Level Authorization (BOLA) in virtual card operations, and exposed debug infrastructure—that pose an immediate threat to the bank's operational integrity.

Compliance Gaps: Current gaps in PCI-DSS controls and data protection practices require urgent attention to maintain regulatory standing.

AI Governance: The integration of AI agents introduces unique risks, requiring a balance between innovation and robust technical guardrails to prevent exploitation.

Strategic Roadmap
Immediate Action (0-48 Hours): Engineering teams are prioritized to remediate the three identified "Critical" risks to stabilize the production environment.

Governance Integration: We are implementing NIST CSF 2.0 and other specialized frameworks (PCI-DSS, ISO/IEC 27001) to build a sustainable, defensible security posture.

Ongoing Oversight: A continuous monitoring and audit cycle has been established to ensure that risk levels remain within the organization’s defined appetite.

Conclusion
While the vuln-bank ecosystem faces significant technical challenges, the implementation of these high-priority controls will fundamentally secure our critical assets. This engagement ensures we are not just fixing vulnerabilities, but establishing a culture of proactive compliance and robust AI governance.
