
### **Engagement Overview**
This six-week corporate compliance and AI governance audit engagement, led by Stephen Adeoye, GRC Professional, was tasked with conducting a full-scale assessment of the vuln-bank Digital Ecosystem. The primary objective of this engagement was to evaluate the platform's current security posture, identify critical vulnerabilities, and provide a comprehensive roadmap for remediation in alignment with established industry frameworks.

### **Key Findings**

The audit identified three "Critical" risks: SQL Injection, Broken Object Level Authorization (BOLA) in virtual card operations, and exposed debug infrastructure, that pose an immediate threat to the bank's operational integrity. Beyond these technical threats, there are significant gaps in PCI-DSS controls and data protection practices that require urgent attention to maintain regulatory standing. Furthermore, the integration of AI agents introduces unique risks, necessitating a careful balance between innovation and the implementation of robust technical guardrails to prevent exploitation.

### **Strategic Roadmap**

To address these findings, our immediate action plan (0-48 hours) prioritizes engineering efforts to remediate the three identified "Critical" risks, thereby stabilizing the production environment. Moving forward, we are integrating NIST CSF 2.0 along with other specialized frameworks, such as PCI-DSS and ISO/IEC 27001, to build a sustainable and defensible security posture. To ensure long-term success, a continuous monitoring and audit cycle has been established to ensure that risk levels remain within the organization’s defined appetite.

### **Conclusion**
While the vuln-bank ecosystem faces significant technical challenges, the implementation of these high-priority controls will fundamentally secure our critical assets. This engagement ensures we are not just fixing vulnerabilities, but establishing a culture of proactive compliance and robust AI governance.
